2. Data protection compliance

CosTrace complies with the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) for users located in the European Union, and applies equivalent protections to users elsewhere, regardless of local requirements.

3. What data we collect

Account data - Full name, email address, password (hashed — we never see or store it in plain text; authentication is handled by Supabase Auth) - Optional: avatar selection, display preferences

Financial / usage data (the core of the app) - Expenses you or your ledger co-members enter: description, amount, currency, date, category, who paid, who owes what - Ledger names, member lists, settlement and payout records - Plan/subscription status (which plan you're on, renewal date)

Payment data - We do not collect or store your card number, CVV, or full billing details ourselves. Payments are processed by Paddle (our payment provider and Merchant of Record) and entitlements are tracked by RevenueCat. We receive only: which plan you bought, the price tier, and a subscription status/expiry date.

Technical data - IP address, browser/device type, basic usage logs (for security, fraud prevention, and debugging) - Push notification tokens, if you enable notifications - Standard authentication cookies/local storage needed to keep you logged in. We do not currently use advertising or analytics cookies. If we introduce ads or analytics in the future, we will update this policy and request consent where required before doing so.

Data from other people - If you're added to a shared ledger, other members' names, avatars, and their entries in that ledger are visible to you as part of how shared expense tracking works. This is inherent to the product — a shared ledger cannot function without shared visibility among its members.

5. Who we share data with

We share the minimum data necessary with the following processors, each of whom is contractually bound to only use your data to provide their service to us:

  • Supabase — our database, authentication, and file storage provider.
  • RevenueCat — tracks which entitlements (plan/add-ons) your account has.
  • Paddle — our payment processor and Merchant of Record; they handle the actual transaction, card data, tax/VAT, and refunds. Paddle has its own privacy policy governing the checkout process.
  • Push notification service (Web Push) — delivers notifications you've opted into.

We do not sell your data. We do not share your data with advertisers or data brokers for marketing purposes, and we have no current plans to. If this ever changes, we will update this policy and, where legally required, ask for your consent first.

6. International data transfers

Because our infrastructure providers (e.g., Supabase, RevenueCat, Paddle) may process or store data outside your home country — including outside the EU/EEA — your data may be transferred internationally. Where we transfer data out of the EU/EEA, we rely on the safeguards required by GDPR (such as Standard Contractual Clauses) as implemented by our processors. If you'd like details of a specific processor's transfer mechanism, contact us.

7. How long we keep your data

We designed our data retention around a clear, honest rule rather than vague language:

  • If you delete your account, it is scheduled for permanent deletion 30 days later. You can cancel the deletion at any time within that window from your Profile screen. After 30 days, this is executed automatically and irreversibly, including deletion of your Supabase Auth login and profile record.
  • Ledgers you solely own (no other real participant) are deleted permanently along with their expense history when your account is deleted.
  • Ledgers you share with others are not deleted when you leave or your account is removed — your name is retained as a historical label on past entries (so shared history stays intact for the remaining members), but it is no longer linked to a live account.
  • If you (as the owner) delete a shared ledger directly, other members are notified immediately and have 3 days to save an archived copy for themselves before it's permanently removed from the original.
  • Your plan tier determines how much expense history is visible to you in the app (e.g., Free shows 3 months, paid tiers show more or unlimited) — this is a display limit, not a deletion; older data is retained per the rules above, not silently erased just because a lower plan can't display it.

8. Your rights

Depending on your location, you may have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you
  • Correct inaccurate data
  • Delete your data ("right to be forgotten") — you can do this yourself, in-app, via Profile → Delete Account
  • Restrict or object to certain processing
  • Port your data to another service in a structured format
  • Withdraw consent at any time (e.g., disabling push notifications)
  • Lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority (in the EU, this is the supervisory authority of your country of residence)

To exercise any of these rights beyond what's available in-app, email support@costrace.app. We will respond within the timeframe required by applicable law (generally one month under GDPR, extendable once for complex requests).

9. Children's privacy

CosTrace is not directed at, and is not intended for use by, children. You must be at least 16 years old (or the age of digital consent in your country, if higher — some EU member states set this between 13 and 16) to use CosTrace without parental consent, and at least 13 in any case, in line with US COPPA requirements. If we learn that we've collected data from a child below the applicable minimum age without appropriate consent, we will delete it promptly. Contact us if you believe this has happened.

10. Security

We rely on industry-standard practices: encrypted connections (HTTPS/TLS) for all data in transit, database-level row-level security policies restricting who can read or write which records, and hashed password storage handled by our authentication provider. No system is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security, but we take reasonable, current measures to protect your data.
